Last Problem I Solved!

Isomorphism between one-to-one functions

Let \(S_1\) and \(S_2\) be two sets. Suppose that there exists a one-to-one mapping \(f\) of \(S_1\) into \(S_2\). Show that there exists an isomorphism of \(A(S_1)\) into \(A(S_2)\), where \(A(S)\) is the set of all one-to-one mappings of \(S\) onto itself.

Solution:

For any function \(g_1\) in \(A(S_1) \), let \( h(g_1) = g_2 \) be a function such that if \(x\) is mapped to \(y\) in \(S_1\) by \(g_1 \), then \(g_2\) maps \(f(x)\) to \(f(y)\) and \(g_2\) maps each element in \(S_2 \setminus f(S_1) \) to itself. It's easy to see that \(h\) is an isomorphism of \(A(S_1)\) into \(A(S_2)\).
